Tribute to Wangari Maathai

About Wangari Maathai

Wangari Maathai (1940-2011) was a Kenyan environmental political activist and the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize. She founded the Green Belt Movement, an environmental organization focused on tree planting, environmental conservation, and women's rights.

Notable Works

  • Founding the Green Belt Movement
  • Recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004
  • Author of "The Green Belt Movement: Sharing the Approach and the Experience"

Impact and Legacy

Wangari Maathai's work has had a lasting impact on environmental conservation, women's empowerment, and sustainable development. Her legacy continues to inspire environmental activists and human rights advocates around the world.
